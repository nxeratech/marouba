## The Problem

### The internet is drowning in AI-generated nothing.

Every tool now has an "AI assist" button. Every platform wants to generate your content for you. The result is a flood of stuff that looks like everything else — because it was made by the same models, trained on the same data, producing the same output.

What's missing is you. Your specific decisions. The way you colour grade. The way you layer sounds. The particular sequence of choices that makes your work recognisably yours.

The AI industry doesn't care about your process. It cares about replacing it. Faster output. More output. Cheaper output. Quality measured in volume.

That's the opposite of what Marouba does.

Marouba doesn't generate anything. It remembers what *you* did — your creative decisions, your preferences, your established process — and makes sure you never have to repeat yourself. It's a value system built into the architecture: your work is yours, your vault is yours, and nobody accesses it without your consent.

## How It Works

### Your process, recorded. Yours forever.

Marouba sits on your machine — Windows or Mac — and watches how you work in any application. Not just ComfyUI. Not just Photoshop. Any app you use. Ableton. Premiere. Excel. Your browser. Anything.

When you do something worth remembering, you tell Marouba to record it. It captures not just the button clicks but the intent behind them — what you were trying to achieve, not just where you pointed your mouse.

That recording becomes a **vault** — a human-readable markdown file that lives on your machine. You own it. You can read it, edit it, version it with git, open it in Obsidian. It's not locked in a database. It's not sent to a cloud. It's yours.

When you need that workflow again, any tool in your stack can call it — your AI agent, a script, a cron job, or the Marouba app itself. Teach once, every tool you run can replay it forever.

If something changed since last time — a button moved, an app updated, a dialog appeared — Marouba fixes it. It reads the vault, figures out what went wrong, finds a path forward, and writes the repair back. It only asks you once. Next time, it already knows.

## The Vault

### Not a macro. Not a script. Not a cloud service. Yours.

Every workflow you teach Marouba is stored as a markdown file. That's it. Plain text. Human readable. You can open it in any editor and understand exactly what it does.

You can edit it. You can version it with git. You can open your entire vault in Obsidian and see your creative process as a knowledge graph — every workflow, every decision, every repair, linked together.

The vault stores:
- **What you did** — the actual steps, with full context
- **What you meant** — the intent behind each decision
- **What the screen looked like** — visual fingerprints for each step
- **What happened when it broke** — every repair, every adaptation
- **Fallback paths** — alternative routes when the main one fails

This isn't data being collected about you. It's data created by you, owned by you, stored on your machine. The vault format is open. The engine is open source (BSL). The architecture is designed so that your creative process stays in your hands — always.

## What Marouba Is Not

Let's be clear about this.

Marouba is not an AI that generates content for you. It's not another "AI assistant" that replaces your decisions with statistical averages. It doesn't make things faster by making them worse.

Marouba is a memory layer. It remembers what you did so you don't have to do it again. It respects your process instead of replacing it. It gives you ownership over how you work instead of renting your workflow back to you as a subscription.

If you want to generate a thousand images that look like everything else on the internet, there are plenty of tools for that. Marouba is for people who care about what they make and want to own every step of how they make it.

## The Details

- **Works with any app** on Windows or Mac — no integrations needed, no plugins to install
- **Runs locally** — nothing leaves your machine unless you choose to share it
- **Vaults are markdown** — human readable, git-friendly, Obsidian-compatible
- **Open source** (BSL licence) — the engine is transparent, auditable, community-driven
- **8-route execution** — always picks the fastest, cheapest way to complete your workflow
- **Self-repairing** — detects breakage, fixes it, writes the fix back, never asks twice
- **Universal call interface** — MCP server, REST API, CLI, or the app itself. Your agents, scripts, and tools all speak to the same vault.
