## The Solution

### Marouba: teach once. Replay forever. Repair itself.

Marouba sits between you and your operating system. When you perform a workflow, it watches, learns, and stores what you did in a structured format called a **vault**.

The vault isn't a macro. It isn't a script. It isn't a screen recording. It's a precise, human-readable, version-controlled description of your intent, your actions, and the context that made them work.

**Four steps. That's it.**

### Teach
Perform your workflow normally. Marouba observes and records the full context — not just what you clicked, but what you meant. The vault captures intent, not just instructions.

### Replay
Need the same workflow again? Marouba replays it from the vault. Different window size, different screen layout — the intent carries over even when the specifics change.

### Repair
Something broke? A button moved, a dialog appeared, an update rearranged the UI? Marouba detects the failure, uses the vault's stored context to find a path forward, repairs the workflow, and writes the fix back. It only asks for help once. The next time it encounters that situation, it already knows the answer.

### Share
Vaults are portable. Teach a workflow on your machine, share it, and it adapts to someone else's. Their apps, their versions, their setup. Workflow knowledge becomes transferable for the first time.

## The Vault

### Not a macro. Not a script. A living record of how you work.

The vault format is what makes Marouba different from every automation tool you've used.

Traditional automations are brittle because they store **actions**: "click at (340, 290), type 'report.pdf', press Enter." Move the button and the whole thing breaks.

Marouba vaults store **intent**: "export the current document as a PDF named with today's date and save it to the Desktop." The specific clicks, coordinates, and keystrokes are implementation details that Marouba handles — and re-handles when they change.

**Each vault contains:**
- **Intent description** — what you're trying to achieve, in plain language
- **Action sequence** — the steps you performed, with full context
- **Screen state snapshots** — what the interface looked like at each step
- **Fallback paths** — alternative approaches when the primary path breaks
- **Repair history** — every time something changed and how it was fixed
- **Adaptation rules** — how the workflow should adjust for different setups

Vaults are YAML + JSON. They're git-friendly — diff them, branch them, version them. They're human-readable — open one in a text editor and understand exactly what it does.

**The 8-route hierarchy:** every vault lives in a lifecycle — teach, replay, verify, repair, adapt, compose, share, report. Each route is a stage in the workflow's journey from your first demonstration to a shared, battle-tested community asset.

## The Marketplace

### Your workflows have value. You should be able to share it.

The Marouba marketplace is where workflow knowledge becomes a creator economy asset.

Built a perfect Photoshop colour grading workflow? Share it — or sell it. Figured out a bulletproof Ableton mastering chain? Package it as a vault. Created an efficient data pipeline in Excel? Someone else needs exactly that.

**How it works:**
- **Publish** your vault to the marketplace with a description and price (or free)
- **Download** vaults from other creators — they auto-adapt to your machine
- **Earn** when someone uses your workflow — creator economy meets automation
- **Rate and review** — the community validates which workflows actually work

The marketplace isn't just distribution. It's a quality signal. Vaults that have been downloaded 10,000 times and repaired successfully across 500 different machines are fundamentally more trustworthy than a workflow someone recorded once.

**The network effect:** every repair, every adaptation, every use makes the vault better. The more people who use a workflow, the more robust it becomes. This is open-source reliability applied to workflow knowledge.
