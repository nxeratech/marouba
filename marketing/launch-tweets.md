## Tweet 1 — The Problem (Monday)

Every AI agent session starts from zero. You teach it to export a report, the app updates, the button moves, and it's gone. Again.

We built Marouba so your computer remembers what you taught it.

Teach once. Replay forever.

## Tweet 2 — How It Works (Tuesday)

The vault is a structured file — not a macro, not a script.

It stores your intent. What you meant to do, not just where you clicked.

When something breaks, the vault has enough context to repair the workflow and write the fix back.

Next time, it already knows.

marouba.app

## Tweet 3 — Open Source (Wednesday)

Marouba is MIT licensed. Runs locally. Your workflows stay on your machine.

No cloud dependency. No vendor lock-in. Your vaults are just files — diff them, branch them, version them with git.

Open source because workflow memory shouldn't belong to one company.

marouba.app

## Tweet 4 — The Repair Loop (Thursday)

Most automations die on their first UI update.

Marouba detects the break, reads the vault for intent and fallback paths, attempts a fix, and saves the result.

~70% of common breakages self-repair. The other 30% it asks once — then remembers forever.

That's the loop.

marouba.app
